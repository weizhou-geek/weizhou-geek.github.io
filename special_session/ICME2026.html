ICME 2026 Special Session
Multimodal Medical AI and Foundation Models for Healthcare Multimedia Systems
Bangkok, Thailand | July 5 - July 9, 2026

Call for Papers

The emergence of large multimodal models and medical foundation models is reshaping intelligent healthcare systems. By leveraging diverse medical data including images, videos, signals, text, and physiological streams, modern AI enables unified analysis, interpretable reasoning, and enhanced clinical decision support.

This special session seeks cutting-edge research on multimodal learning, foundation models, and AI-driven medical multimedia systems. Submissions addressing new algorithms, system frameworks, datasets, and real-world clinical applications are highly encouraged. Key topics of interest in this session include, but are not limited to:

  • Multimodal learning and cross-modal representation for medical data
  • Foundation models and LMMs for medical multimedia analysis
  • Zero-shot / few-shot / self-supervised learning in healthcare
  • Medical report generation and vision-language models
  • Wearable sensor data understanding and multimodal fusion
  • Temporal / spatiotemporal medical video analysis
  • Explainability, bias mitigation, and trustworthy medical AI
  • Clinical workflow integration and deployment of multimodal systems

Paper Submission

Submission website: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/IEEEICME2026

After signing in the ICME 2026 submission site as the author, please choose our Special Session name to submit the paper. Papers must be no longer than 6 pages, including all text, figures, and references. ICME 2026 reviewing is double blind, which means that authors cannot know the names of the reviewers of their papers, and reviewers cannot know the names of the authors. Information that may identify the authors anywhere in the submitted materials must be avoided. In particular, in the submitted pdf paper, the usual list of authors, their institutions, and their contact information must be replaced by the phrase, "Anonymous ICME Submission." Identifying information in the acknowledgments (e.g., co-workers and grant IDs), supplemental materials (e.g., titles in the videos, or attached papers), and links to the authors’ or their institutions’ websites must also be avoided.

Please refer to the main conference site for more submission policies on blinding, supplemental material, presentation guarantee, etc.
